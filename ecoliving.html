Weekly Waste Sorting Tips

Plastic

Thoroughly rinse and ensure plastic bottles and containers are fully dry to prevent contamination. Carefully examine items for recycling symbols . Actively reduce your plastic use by choosing reusable alternatives to minimize waste generation at the source.

Organic Waste

Investigate home composting for food peels, coffee grounds, and garden trimmings to create valuable soil. Inquire with Mombasa authorities about designated organic waste collection programs, including accepted materials and proper sorting methods. Separating organics reduces landfill burden and potential methane emissions.

E-waste

Never discard old electronics with regular trash due to hazardous materials. Seek out specific e-waste collection centers or designated drop-off events in Mombasa. These ensure safe recycling and prevent environmental contamination. Remember to wipe personal data before disposal.

Paper & Cardboard

Ensure all paper and cardboard are free from food stains and moisture to maintain recyclability. Flatten cardboard boxes to conserve space in collection bins and during transport. Clean, dry, and flattened paper maximizes recycling efficiency in Mombasa's waste management system.

DIY Pallet Furniture Tutorial

Upcycling wooden pallets can transform discarded materials into functional and stylish furniture. Here's a basic guide:

Step 1: Source and Prepare Pallets

Find clean, dry, and untreated pallets. Inspect them for nails, staples, or damage. Sand down rough surfaces and edges to prevent splinters.

Step 2: Design Your Furniture

Decide what you want to create (e.g., coffee table, bench, vertical garden). Sketch your design and plan your cuts if necessary.

Step 3: Cut and Assemble

If your design requires cutting, use appropriate tools (saw). Assemble the pallet pieces using screws, nails, or strong adhesive, depending on your project.

Step 4: Finish and Decorate

Paint, stain, or varnish your pallet furniture for protection and aesthetics. Add cushions, wheels, or other decorative elements to personalize your creation.

For more detailed tutorials and inspiration, search online for "DIY pallet furniture projects".

Turning Old Clothes into Bags Tutorial

Give your unwanted clothes a new purpose by upcycling them into unique bags. Here's a simple approach:

Step 1: Choose Your Fabric

Select sturdy fabric from old jeans, t-shirts, skirts, or jackets. Consider the size and type of bag you want to make.

Step 2: Cut the Fabric

Cut the fabric into the desired shape and size for your bag. Remember to account for seams and handles.

Step 3: Sew the Bag

Sew the edges of the fabric together to form the main body of the bag. You can use a sewing machine or hand-stitch for this step.

Step 4: Add Handles or Straps

Create handles or straps from leftover fabric, belts, or other materials. Attach them securely to the bag.

Step 5: Personalize Your Bag

Add buttons, zippers, pockets, or other embellishments to customize your upcycled bag.

Numerous online tutorials offer detailed instructions for various bag styles made from old clothing. Search for "DIY bag from old clothes".

Creative Packaging Reuse Tutorial

Transform everyday packaging into useful and decorative items with a little creativity:

Step 1: Glass Jars

Clean glass jars thoroughly. Use them for storing spices, pantry staples, craft supplies, or as decorative vases. Paint them, wrap them with twine, or add labels for a stylish look.

Step 2: Cardboard Boxes

Cardboard boxes can be repurposed as drawer organizers, storage containers, or even as building blocks for kids. Decorate them with wrapping paper, fabric scraps, or paint.

Step 3: Plastic Bottles

Cut plastic bottles to create planters for small herbs or succulents. The bottom parts can also serve as small storage containers for stationery or other items.

Step 4: Fabric Scraps

Leftover fabric from packaging or old clothes can be used to make reusable shopping bags, cleaning cloths, or patches.

Explore online resources for "creative packaging upcycling ideas" to discover a wealth of projects.

Stylish Glass Jar Storage Tutorial

Step 1: Clean the Jars

Wash the glass jars thoroughly with soap and hot water. Remove any labels.

Step 2: Decorate the Jars

Get creative with decorations! You can paint them, wrap them with twine or lace, decoupage them, or use glass etching.

Step 3: Create Lids (Optional)

You can decorate the existing lids or create new ones from fabric or wood.

Step 4: Organize Your Items

Use your stylish glass jars to store anything from pantry staples to craft supplies.
